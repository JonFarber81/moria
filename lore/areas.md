# Moria — Areas & Named Locations (Gazetteer)

> Source: *TOR2 — Moria: Through the Doors of Durin*, Ch. 5. Lore/flavor only. This is the master reference for **naming and theming dungeon levels** as the player descends. Ordered surface → deepest.

## Vertical Structure (the depth system)

Moria is reckoned from the **East-gate on the First Level**:
- **Levels** = floors *above* the East-gate (2nd–7th → the Mountain Galleries and peaks; open to sky/air).
- **Deeps** = floors *below* the East-gate (First Deep, Second Deep … → the mines and the unplumbed abyss where Durin's Bane dwells).
- **Halls** are numbered by *order of construction*, not location (e.g. "the Twenty-First Hall on the Seventh Level"). Internal fortress-**Gates** guard key passages.

**Landmark discoverability tiers:** *Famous* (any Dwarf knows), *Obscure* (forgotten, found by chance/research), *Hidden* (secret).

### Suggested depth ladder for the roguelike (surface → deepest)
| Band | Region / Landmarks |
|---|---|
| Approaches (surface) | **Dimrill Dale** (Azanulbizar), Dimrill Stair, Mirrormere, **East-gate** |
| Peaks / upper Levels | **Mountains of Moria** — Citadel of Barazinbar, Mountain Galleries, Durin's Tower |
| First Level / entry | **Old Moria** — First Hall (Durin's Hall), Second Hall & Durin's Bridge, the Last Redoubt |
| Upper city | **The Dwarrowdelf** — Caves of Thrym Thistlebeard, King's Hall, Chamber of Mazarbul, Vault of Uruktharbun, Fortress of Malech |
| The long road | **The Road** — Halls of the Roadwardens, Great West Cave (Tomb of Durin) |
| Far west | **West Moria (Dol Mírdan)** — Imperishable Hall, Lost Citadel of the Ring-smiths, Doors of Durin |
| The Deeps | Armouries of the Third Deep, Goblin Village, Ledge of Woe, **Udûn-temple** |
| The Mines (climax) | Den of Forgotten Stars, **The Balrog's Throne**, Foundations of Stone |

**How the Dwarves delved (flavor vocabulary):** *Wind & Water* (aqueducts, cisterns, air-shafts with mechanical fans and circulating fires, forge-vents blackening the snow); *Light* (enchanted crystal lamps in great halls, torches in small chambers, mirror-shafts carrying daylight deep); *Food* (farms in Dimrill Dale & Hollin, mountain goats, famed storehouses — "busy Dwarves can forget to eat for days"); *Ways In & Out* (only the East-gate is easy to find).

**Aesthetic eras (to date a level's look):** oldest/austere (low ceilings, few statues, Durin's restraint) → richly-decorated middle city (runes, gems, gilt) → Elf-influenced (crystal, light, tree-carved pillars) → fearful era (traps, secret ways over beauty) → decline (abandoned sections after the plague of 1636).

# APPROACHES — The Dimrill Dale

**Names:** *Azanulbizar* (Khuzdul), *Nanduhirion* (Sindarin), *Dimrill Dale* (Common). The eastern approach; "once a crossroads in Middle-earth," now eerily empty — wild goats, sinister rats, circling carrion-birds, returning Warg-packs, and almost treeless (the last forests cut for funeral pyres after the Battle of Azanulbizar).

- **Crows of Dimrill Dale** — malicious, taste for Dwarf-flesh, speak some Black Speech, spy for Orcs. **Mocker Crawe** — the oldest/biggest crow, big enough to carry off a lamb, a false messenger between Orc-tribes and Dol Guldur/Mordor, hoards jewels "like a tiny dragon's hoard," mocks all in satirical rhyme.

### The Dimrill Stair *(famous)*
A grand Dwarf-built stone stair winding behind and through a cascade of waterfalls up toward the Redhorn Pass; colonnaded, adorned with images of Dwarven heroes. "A promise that greater wonders still lie beneath the mountain."
- **Dimrill Watch** — ruined fortress, its secret tunnels to the city destroyed; now used by travellers or lurking Orcs.
- **The Mirrormere (Kheled-zâram)** — dark, windless, glass-still sacred lake. Durin saw a crown of stars reflected above his head; it shows sky and Durin's crown in unreachable deeps but **does not reflect those who look into it** — perhaps "a window into the other side." A simple pillar marks where Durin first looked. Place of Dwarven pilgrimage.
- **The Dimrill-Door (17th Pillar)** *(hidden)* — a Dwarf-hidden postern into the lower city, openable only by a **Mithril key** (captured by Orcs, sent to Sauron, now used by Mordor-orcs to slip in unseen).

### The Mounds of Azanulbizar *(obscure)*
Grassy Orc burial mounds around the old battlefield — "a corrupt and haunted place; it is not good to rest here." Resting brings whispering "like the rustling of ashes."
- **The Central Stone** — an Orc monument, actually dragged out of Moria (it once adorned the doorway of the **First Hall**), painted with the sign of Bolg, then Gorgol's severed bear's head.
- **The Gibbet** — where Thrór's and Azog's remains were displayed; now holds a fresh Orc body bearing the **Lidless Eye** (an unwelcome Mordor messenger).

### The East-gate of Moria *(famous)*
"A wonder of the world even in desolation." A tall stone arch "so huge it could swallow Ancalagon the Black," graven with Khuzdul spells of prohibition and inscriptions in many tongues commanding all to depart who lack "the leave of the Lord of Moria." Where Thrór was slain. **No army ever breached it from outside — "only from within could Khazad-dûm fall."**
- Gates iron-girded in Mithril, perfectly balanced; locking mechanism destroyed, Orcs use Stone-trolls to bar them.
- **Gorlakh the Doorwarden** — an old Orc who wears a wide-brimmed hat against the sun; keeps the ceremonial Gate-key, Moria coins, and Mordor amulets. Watched over by Eagles (**Thoradel**, the scholar **Taentirith** who mapped Moria's air-shafts, and the captive **Padroval** whose cries echo from within).

# ENTRY — Old Moria

The oldest, most defiled part of the city, nearest the surface; where Durin first labored and the last Dwarves held out ~1 year against Durin's Bane. Scars of the final battle: flames "intense enough to melt stone," "a faint, impossible stench of burnt flesh, and the shadows are thick with ghosts."

### The First Hall — Durin's Hall *(famous)*
Durin's original hall, carved by the Father of the Longbeards with his memories of the Elder Days (even the face of Aulë). Preserved as a coronation shrine.
- **The Outer Hall** — every original chisel-mark preserved; its Ithildin/gold monument stripped and covered in Orc filth.
- **The Inner Hall** — coronation throne still stands, but the carvings were hacked away and hidden by King Náin I as the city fell (the hiding place lost; Dwarves feel Dread here). Kings' names Durin I→V inscribed near the throne; Náin I's name hastily scratched in the floor with numeral **V** (a clue to the Fifth Hall / Last Redoubt).

### The Second Hall and Durin's Bridge *(famous)*
- **Durin's Bridge** — a narrow curving 50-foot span, no rail, over a **bottomless chasm** ("never plumbed and may be bottomless"); one warrior can hold it against a host.
- **The Second Hall** — "a true wonder of the world," lines of huge black pillars carved like trees vanishing into dark (some hollow, hiding secret stairs); a great crossroads up into the Dwarrowdelf and down into the Deeps.
- **The Stairs of Sun and Moon** — half silver-paneled, half gold, with sculpted silver and gold trees — **melted to slag by the Balrog's passing.**
- **The Shrine of Azog** — a side room holding **Azog's skull**, coins in its mouth, haunted by his malice.

### The Last Redoubt of the Dwarves — the Fifth Hall *(hidden)*
The final stand. When Durin's Bane awoke (T.A. 1980), **King Náin I** made the Fifth Hall his throne room, entrusted the chief of the **Seven Rings** to his son Thráin I and sent him to safety, then challenged the Balrog — which came as a burning cloud, drove them back, and **sealed the doors with a spell**, trapping Náin and his folk to starve. Unfound in 1,000 years.
- **The Fifth Hall** — "a sight of horror"; skeletons of Náin's bodyguards; warning-signs they drew of Durin's Bane, that no weapon can wound it; a great arsenal of Dwarven arms.
- **The Cairn of King Náin** — holds a heavy **Ring of Keys** (a Mithril circle of keys that unlocks all the Doors of the Dwarrowdelf, the right key always to hand, unlosable; looking through the circle reveals hidden doors).

# UPPER CITY — The Dwarrowdelf

Durin's capital — "of old not darksome, but full of light and splendour," now "all one great tomb." A labyrinth where the fight against the Balrog was fiercest; halls high enough for clouds to form within; caverns "like an underground parkland, with stalagmites instead of trees."

### The Caves of Thrym Thistlebeard *(famous)* — Second Deep
"The Dwarves built a garden under the mountain!" A vast natural cavern made a grand park — tree-sized mushrooms, greenswards of moss "beneath a stone sky," follies and herb-gardens for healing. Now near-lightless, partly flooded; blind cave fish swim the stagnant pools. "A good place to rest and hide... until the twilight and the constant dripping of water drives you mad."
- **Tower of Thrym & Thrym's Tomb** — King Thrym entombed seated overlooking his gardens, in a hollow statue with a gap in the chin for a thistle to grow — but a blood-drinking **Blood-vine** took root instead. Holds **Thrym's Eye** (a sapphire that grants far-sight).
- **The Last Lamps of Moria** — two still-glowing magical lamps (one moonlight, one sunlight); surface plants thrive in this "blessed alcove."
- **House of Healing** — ancient Dwarven surgery amid living things; keenest scalpels, old anatomical texts.
- **Goblin-Ladder** — the route down to Goblin Village.

### The King's Hall *(famous)*
The royal throne room (built by Durin II, seat until Durin VI). A Mithril-and-gold throne under a black-marble dome studded with golden stars; "harps never silent." The Orcs fear it — the **death-curse of Durin VI** lies upon it.
- **King's Hall** — untouched by Orcs, gold still glitters; but Dwarf-skeletons line the walls, a huge burn consumes the silver floor, and of King Durin VI on his toppled scorched throne "nothing remains but ash." All who enter are cursed.
- **Hall of Seven Thrones** — seven seats for the seven Dwarf-lineages; violence forbidden by unbreakable tradition (binding even creatures of Shadow). Legend: a door here leads to the **Endless Stair**, to open "when the Seven are gathered again."
- **Hall of Judgement (the Eighth Hall)** — where kings sentenced criminals; the worst punishment was **banishment** — the exiled made "houseless and nameless," their secret Khuzdul names stripped onto **lead plates** vaulted in the floor. Orcs made it a fighting-pit.

### The Chamber of Mazarbul *(obscure)* — later Balin's seat and tomb
The great record-hall/archive on the Fifth Level. Dwarves were "diligent, precise... stony hearts set aflame by great ledgers of accounts." Held records of **all the great civic buildings and fortifications**.
- **The Twenty-first Hall** — great cavern near the Road; polished black walls "smooth as glass," a pale beam of morning light from an eastern shaft. Contested by Orc-bands (currently held by **Hagrot the Sneak**, nephew of Granny Goblin).
- **The Chamber of Mazarbul** — wall-niches of iron-bound chests of scrolls; records largely intact; a stone door with an iron ring "could hold back a Cave-troll — for a little while." (Site of the Fellowship's canonical fight; Balin's tomb.)

### The Vault of Uruktharbun *(obscure)* — the Treasure Vault
A lightless labyrinth in "nigh-impenetrable granite at the heart of the Silvertine," filled with **profound darkness no light can pierce (save perhaps a Silmaril)** and cunning death-traps. Its hereditary **vault-keepers** (trained from youth to navigate the dark, some growing beards "like a spool of thread" to find their way back) all perished when the Balrog rose. Orcs bored it full of random tunnels, "like an apple gnawed by innumerable worms."
- **Dwarven traps:** concealed pits (clay over a hole), false doors (drop a stone block), one-way doors (slam and trap you).
- **Angolath's Lair** — a gigantic centipede (spawned in Thangorodrim, carried by the Balrog) whose poisonous hairs coat everything in toxic dust.
- **Mithril Vault** — trade-Mithril and a few True Mithril ingots; **King's Coffers** (a Marvellous Hoard cursed so stolen gold brings despair); **Vault of Treasures** (fabled relics — a singing harp, a crystal sphere by Aulë showing Arda Perfected — or a decoy death-trap).

### The Fortress of Malech — the Palace of Stairs *(obscure)*
A minor Dwarven palace built into a huge cavern wall, now the paranoid lair of the Orc Great-Chieftain. "Staircases ran up the wall, and great galleries looked out upon the cave-city below." Riddled with secret passages, guard-posts, passwords.
- **Cave of Lights** — a cavern that "could swallow the palaces of Arnor, Gondor, Númenor"; a **Broken Lamp** that once blazed the whole cave (relighting it would dismay the Orcs like sunlight).
- **Yagul the Snatcher** — Malech's right hand, master of the slave-pits ("the Orc-snatcher will get you!"); **Brute Uftak** — his massive half-Troll enforcer; **Imri's Forge** — where the traitor Dwarf Imri makes weapons but can't master Mithril.
- **Malech One-eye** — old Orc, empty socket filled with **molten gold**, sole survivor of Bolg's honor guard at Five Armies, now utterly paranoid ("the only thing that waits for him in the dark is a knife"). His notes decode every named landmark of Moria.

# THE PEAKS — The Mountains of Moria

The upper Levels — towers and castles in the peaks, "the Dwarves climbed as well as delved." Built on Silvertine (Zirakzigil/Celebdil) and Cloudyhead; used mainly in summer. Now "little remains save piles of fallen stones buried beneath snow-drifts." Occupied by cold-hating Mordor-orcs; Trolls again roam Caradhras.

### The Redhorn Gate and the Citadel of Barazinbar *(famous)*
The mountain pass (**Caradhras**, the Redhorn) and its ruined keeper-fortress, now so wrecked that travellers "mistake it for Khazad-dûm itself," unaware the far greater ruin lies beneath their feet.
- **The Scar of Celebrían** — where Celebrían was waylaid and tormented by Moria-orcs (T.A. 2509); her sons Elladan and Elrohir hunt Orcs here.
- **Red-Nails' House** — a cave-mouth haunted by the vampire-spirit **Red-Nails** (a corpse-pale old woman in white who eats frozen blood); "a tempting place to shelter in a storm."
- **Ghost-Dogs of the Redhorn Pass** — spectral Dwarf-bred rescue-dogs said to still aid lost travellers.

### The Mountain Galleries *(obscure)* — the uppermost Levels
The "airiest and most beautiful" halls, grand terraces overlooking Dimrill Dale and the Anduin, preferred by Elf-visitors from Hollin. Their connecting stairs were collapsed when the Balrog took the city; unvisited by Dwarves in 1,000 years. Now the lair of the traitor Dwarf **Har** and the Uruk **Naglur**.
- **The Great Gallery** — the one hall kept intact; iron pillars cast like flowering trees; a staggering view where "the Golden Wood glitters brighter than any gold"; a carved wall-map of Moria showing the **Citadel of the Ring-smiths**.
- **The High Cistern** — a huge pillared water-tank; a hero could lift a plug-block to open a channel into Moria, but a single snow-melt could flood and wash the party away "like spiders down a plughole" — "an entrance that's more of a deathtrap."
- **The Secret Cleft** *(hidden)* — behind a Dwarf-door visible only on Durin's Day; holds **four carved seated Dwarf-kings** (who came "to seek counsel from the distant past"); a rear door leads to the **Endless Stair**.
- **Durin's Tower** — carved into the pinnacle of Celebdil the White, virtually inaccessible; "rises above the airs where mortal things live... where only spirits may dwell." One who climbs here "may ask a single question and be assured of an answer."
- **Spirits of Shadow** — cold servants of the Enemy on the tallest peaks, whirling shapes of snow and ash "like dancing, leaping figures," that drain life to leave frozen husks.

# THE LONG ROAD

The **Low Road** runs ~40 miles under the mountain, West-gate to East-gate — never one straight path but knitted from mine-sections, then deliberately *unpicked* after Eregion fell so only a labyrinth of guarded side-tunnels remained. This secrecy became tragedy: when Durin's Bane arose, most Dwarves couldn't find their way out and perished.

### The Halls of the Roadwardens *(obscure)*
Three guardian halls along the Road (the Straight Road, the Upper Mines, the Stairs), each Roadwarden bearing a lost Mithril staff said to command any door on the Road.
- **The Straight Road** — the Road as dreamed: wide, arrow-straight, snug ashlar masonry "dry as a Hobbit-hole" after a thousand years; scattered old mine-carts.
- **Hall of the First Roadwarden** — one of the best-preserved halls in Moria; feels "as if a great host of travellers departed only moments ago — one can almost hear Dwarven marching songs fading down the road."
- **Hall of the Second Roadwarden** — carved into the inside of a titanic stalactite; cramped but perfectly defensible.
- **Hall of the Third Roadwarden** — flooded with icy stagnant water by a cracked aqueduct; the Warden's dais rises "like a lonely island"; Orcs claim a many-tentacled monster watches from the water.

### The Great West Cave *(obscure)*
One of the wonders of Moria — legend says **Durin I** wrestled a monster (a Dragon or fiery spirit) here, shattering the mountain and rending the cave; said to be where the first Mithril-veins were found.
- **Nan and Gilim** — two massive elevator platforms on a chain-counterweight system; Nan dangles over the abyss, five chains snapped, still loaded with a fortune of Mithril-ore for the brave.
- **Pillars of Fáin** — a shrine-graveyard of crystal-flecked stalagmites that "blaze like the rising sun in torchlight," where Dwarven hermits came to die and be entombed by the growing stone.
- **The Heart of the Mountain — Tomb of Durin** — a secret cave holding a black stone coffin runed *DURIN / FIRST TO AWAKE / FATHER OF HIS FOLK / LORD OF KHAZAD-DÛM*, which no tool can mark or open until Durin wakes again. A place of power; no adversary will trouble a company here (though Durin's Bane could overcome the ward). A Dwarf who sleeps here receives a prophetic dream.
- **The Stone Ghost** — an invisible spirit beyond the Pillars that hurls boulders to crush trespassers; feared by Moria-orcs, worshipped by the Orcs of Udûn.

# THE FAR WEST — West Moria (Dol Mírdan)

**Dol Mírdan**, "the Hill of Smiths" — a stronghold where Dwarves and Elves met in friendship and made wonders, famed for the Master-smith **Narvi** (greatest Dwarven metallurgist since Telchar of Nogrod). After Eregion fell, West Moria turned inward, jealously guarding its dwindling wealth; its lords grew strange.

### The Imperishable Hall *(obscure)* — the Fiftieth Hall
Seat of the Lords of West Moria, named for walls of crystal mirrors backed by **Ithildin**, so a single lamp's light "reflected and refracted endlessly to illuminate the whole hall" — Dwarves held no evil could enter a place so blessed with light. In T.A. 1980 its ruler **Nyi, Daughter of Nithi**, sealed the Gates as survivors fled — dooming others still on the Road — then styled herself Queen of Moria and withheld troops from King Náin. The jewelled panels are now gone, removed "without violence, as if by many skilled craftsmen."
- **The Chambers of Narvi** — preserved as Narvi left them at his death; he provided the Mithril from which Celebrimbor's smiths forged the Rings of Power. After Celebrimbor's loss "his mind was overthrown but his genius remained"; he wrought wonders in obsessive frenzy, sowing traps and riddles, and died at his workbench.
- **The Forge of Narvi** — locked behind an enchanted black-stone door that shows a holly-tree in Ithildin only to one who speaks High-elven, and opens only to the password **Celebrimbor**. Beyond: tools to refine every variety of Mithril.
- **The Rings of Nyi** — cursed rings that overwrite the wearer's mind until they *become* the dead queen (replacement, not possession); at least three "Shadows of Nyi" have arisen in a millennium.

### The Lost Citadel of the Ring-smiths *(hidden)*
A secret forge-citadel of Dwarven ring-makers who gathered "more ring-lore than existed anywhere in Middle-earth." Ring-making drained the smiths of vitality, turning them into cold **Ring-wights**; they worked to the end (and beyond) hoping to forge a ring that could bind Durin's Bane. Kept utterly secret, all doors hidden with runes of concealment.
- **The Door of the Ring-smiths** — a blank stone wall; against it lies the skeleton of a tall Man who with his last strength scratched a circle and a rune. Tap a magic ring to it and words appear: *THIS YOU ARE AND TO THIS YOU RETURN* — the answer, in secret Khuzdul, is "**stone**." Opening it is a corrupting act.
- **Hall of the Ring-smiths** — "Khazad-dûm at the height of its wealth": unbroken crystal lamps over black marble, twisted pillars under a roof of silver, **seven great thrones** around a small Mithril plinth where rings were laid. Dwarves lived here to the very end — food and clothing remain, but **no skeletons**.
- **The Workshops** — one still occupied: Master-smith **Fraegr** slumps over her bench, the unfinished **Last Ring** in her skeletal hand, three rubies waiting to be set — intended as a weapon against the Balrog, to imbue its wearer with protection against fire and darkness.
- **The Library** — the *Annals of the Gwaith-i-Mirdáin* (Jewel-smiths): first-hand accounts of the making of the Rings, including tales told by Celebrimbor and the "Lord of Gifts" (Sauron). "Remarkably dry and flammable."
- **The Vaults** — where Ring-wights are interred (a living intruder wakes them to claw from their caskets); a Greater Hoard whose wondrous items are, of course, **rings**.
- *Meant to be visited once — a place of wonder and horror — not reclaimed. Linger, and Durin's Bane comes.*

### The Doors of Durin *(famous)* — the West-gate
Flanked by two holly-trees (gifts of Eregion); invisible when shut, inlaid with Ithildin that shows in starlight when touched by an Elf or Elf-friend; graven with the two trees and crescent moons, the Star of Fëanor, the Dwarves' anvil-and-hammer, and Durin's crown. Unbreakable by any force left in Middle-earth. Inscription: *"The Doors of Durin, Lord of Moria. Speak, friend, and enter... I, Narvi, made them. Celebrimbor of Hollin drew these signs."*
- **The Camp of the Stair Falls** — a Dwarven pilgrimage campsite by the Sirannon; peddlers sell genuine Moria-relics; home to the scholar-trader **Hornburi Whitebeard**, who studies Moria as it *now* is (and knows "deeper still, some terrible danger").
- **The Mansions of Thráin I** — plain cottages where young Thráin I brooded on retaking Moria; here, toying with his father's gold ring, he recalled a mining colony far east — which became **Erebor**.

# THE DEEPS

Beneath the city — the Upper Deeps held the forges (smithies, refineries, factories "on a scale unimaginable outside Númenor"); below, the old Lower Deeps and the mines. The city above was planned; the city below is "the most confusing and endless labyrinth in all of Middle-earth," tracing exhausted ore-veins.

**Slag-worms** — where foundries were running when the Balrog rose, its heat melted stone and steel into congealed "slag-forms" resembling "twisted dragon-shapes vomited from the tormented earth." They cannot move — but Orcs tell of "releasing" one that toppled and crushed an intruder. A bad omen.

### The Armouries of the Third Deep *(obscure)*
The heart of the Dwarven arsenal, defended by the elite **Tunnel-guards** (enchanted mattocks, Moria-silver shields, trained to fight in darkness). When Durin's Bane awoke they gave their lives in the battle of the **Poisoned Halls** — which now protect the intact Armouries with their lingering poison.
- **The Broken Hall** — a museum of the weapons of the fallen, backed by murals of heroic deeds — now toppled and defaced, overpainted with mocking Orc-images of Dwarven *defeats* (the death of Thrór, the fall of Moria).
- **The Hall of the Mauler** — lair of a gigantic armored Great Cave-troll that, having felt pain for the first time at the Battle of Dimrill Dale, obsessively straps "an absurd pile of ironmongery over every inch of its hide."
- **The Poisoned Halls** — the flower of Dwarven knighthood still lies where it fell, "as if sleeping, perfectly preserved by the poison."
- **The Lower Armoury** — the martial treasures of the Dwarves. If **Durin's Axe** survives, it is here — forged in the Elder Days by Aulë, weapon of the Kings.

### Goblin Village *(hidden — but every Goblin knows it)*
Moria's only near-neutral settlement, in an old Dwarven market square in the Lower Deeps, riddled with tunnels down to the Mines and north to Goblin Town/Gundabad.
- **The Market Square** — fires burn constantly, roasting unidentifiable meat; young Goblins cavort in the flickering shadows; trades in Mithril, slaves, mercenaries, mushrooms, bats, pale cave-fish. Run by **Granny Goblin**, an ancient, disturbingly friendly matriarch (a "murderous Lobelia Sackville-Baggins"), a talented healer and brewer who plays the three factions against each other.
- **The Houses of the Exiles** — burrows built by the resentful exiled nobles of ruined Nogrod and Belegost, now held by Goblins who mockingly keep the old house-names (a Goblin may introduce himself as "Grishnar of the House of the Firebeards").
- **The Invisible Goblins** — three Goblins who found three lesser gold rings of invisibility in West Moria and became unseen thieves-assassins; the rings pass down the line; they now dwell here as unknown guardians (only Granny knows the current keepers).

### The Ledge of Woe *(obscure)*
An Orc "prison without bars": far below Durin's Bridge, a narrow ledge over the bottomless chasm, barred by a crude iron gate. Prisoners are thrust onto it while Orcs bet how long each lasts before hunger, cold, and the dark drive them to leap. Their cries echo up the chasm to the Second Hall far above.
- **The Wailing Stairs** — a long steep road down to the active Mines held by Malech; slave-laborers are marched down it, "their weeping and wailing giving the stairs their name." More than a day to descend.

### The Udûn-temple *(hidden)*
Temple-fortress of the Balrog-worshipping Orcs of Udûn. **No rumour of it has ever reached the outside world; question any denizen and you learn one word — *Ghâsh* (fire).** A ziggurat in a vast cavern, its open top rolling smoke and foul fumes down its sides to pool on the floor; narrow elevated walkways zig-zag above the reek.
- **The Reeking Cave** — the fume-shrouded floor; yellow cave-spiders "the size of a Goblin's head" spin webs "as thick as a Dwarf's beard."
- **Temple Entrances** — three arches, each bearing the same crude drawing of Durin's Bane, "a humanoid figure greater than a Man with a flaming mane, its body drawn in black pitch, its fiery hair smeared blood."
- **The Chamber of Fire** — built around a well whose shaft plunges to a cavern near the Balrog's lair; when the Balrog passes, flame and smoke rise up it. The Balrog "can ascend the shaft on wings of fire" — and a hero could descend it to reach the Lair.
- **Thu the Firespeaker** — high priest, "horribly burned, his hide scorched scaly like a log left in embers, his eyes burning like fiery sparks," his voice "like ash cracking inside his throat." Claims the Balrog speaks to him through the flames. His overriding aim: keep Moria secret — intruders must "perish in silence and darkness."
- **Azor** — not a name but a *title*: Thu's potion grants an Orc great strength and speed while burning them up from within — to be chosen as Azor is to die as a living sacrifice.

# THE MINES — the deepest delving (climax)

Chasing the Mithril-veins under Caradhras; natural perils abound (cave-ins, flooding, foul air); a thousand years of neglect has choked the great pumps and air-shafts. The Orcs dare not enter the lowest portions where Durin's Bane dwells.

### The Den of Forgotten Stars *(hidden)*
Where Mithril was first found — a tremendous cavern, its ore-machinery destroyed by the Balrog's wrath, now a slave-pen. Perversely beautiful: fragments of Mithril in the walls reflect torchlight "like a brilliant sky of stars." Escape is easy — but to where?
- **The Black Barbican** — a fortified guardhouse of jagged Orc-work holding the foot of the Wailing Stairs, with a kennel of Wargs to hunt escaped slaves; ruled by **Shagram** the "Merry Fellow," "the happiest and most sadistic Orc in Moria," who sings as he whips slaves.
- **The Goblin Way** — a maze "dug over thousands of years by jealous, paranoid, crazed Goblins," full of traps and blind corridors; haunted by **Old Scratch**, an ancient Goblin who eats raw fish off silver plates in a lair of salvaged fine furniture, and who has seen Durin's Bane and fears it.
- **To the Lesser Vein and the Foundations of Stone** — a mine-tunnel, silver Mithril-traceries still in the wall, sloping down to a deeper shaft amid the shattered remains of what may have been a **stone seal**, coated in greasy soot. **This is where the Dwarves delved too deep. This is where the Balrog woke.** Those who enter feel a compulsion to climb down toward the fires "like moths to a flame."
  - *Remaking the Seal:* the fragments bear the Tengwar of Rúmil (an ancient Elvish mode few can read), inlaid with an unknown enchanted metal (Ithildin might substitute). Restoring it might re-imprison the Balrog — but someone would have to lure it back down and be "trapped there in the dark forever."

### The Balrog's Throne *(hidden)* — the final depth
The chambers Durin's Bane has claimed. **No lore speaks of this stronghold of darkness.** Not natural caverns — they resemble "an ancient temple or fortress of Morgoth," whether older than the Balrog or carved by the last enslaved Dwarves for their new master. **The darkness is overwhelming — all lights fail; only magical light pierces it. Flames burn but shed no illumination; trespassers must navigate by touch, and the rock walls are hot enough to sear flesh. No one has entered and returned, save Thu the Firespeaker.**
- **Paths of Approach** — all slope steeply down; the incline "seems to suddenly increase the moment intruders think of leaving."
- **Burning Caverns** — pools of molten lava under a scab-like crust; spikes of black basalt as stepping-stones; the Balrog crosses fearlessly, others must leap or burn.
- **The Audience Chamber** — a former underground lake, walls scaled with mineral deposits from the Balrog's steam-heat; a floor of ash, burnt bones, and scorched offerings hurled down by the Orcs of Udûn.
- **The Temple to Darkness** — darkness "as deep as the timeless time before Elbereth kindled the first stars"; nightmare carvings; the echo of distant discordant music "that gnaws at hope and sanity." (Only the **Phial of Galadriel** might drive back this Shadow.)
- **The Balrog's Throne** — a throne of reddish-black basalt "carved from the roots of Caradhras," amid pools of lava; those who sit on it feel themselves "falling out of Arda into the endless void."

### Foundations of Stone — the uttermost deep
The **Foundations of Stone / Gnawed Tunnels / The Lakes Below** — tunnels *gnawed*, not carved, by long-vanished creatures; unmappable. Home to the **Nameless Things**: *"Far, far below the deepest delvings of the Dwarves, the world is gnawed by nameless things. Even Sauron knows them not. They are older than he."*
- **The Endless Stair** — a secret path from Durin's Tower atop the mountain all the way down to Moria's lowest levels; steps circle a self-glimmering pillar, walls carved with runic prayers to **Aulë the Smith**; it was delved first, and the city built around it.
- **The Mithril-veins** — beyond the reach of Man or Dwarf, veins "of surpassing fruitfulness," untapped and immeasurable.

## Reusable area-entry dread cues
Fading torchlight that reveals nothing • echoes of Dwarven marching songs down an empty road • a distant tapping (a Tapper) • "*Ghâsh*" whispered in the dark • slag-worms like twisted dragon-shapes • Mithril-flecks glittering like forgotten stars • light that sheds no illumination • walls hot enough to sear • the floor growing warmer as you rest • a compulsion drawing you downward "like moths to a flame" • drums in the deep — *"They are coming."*
