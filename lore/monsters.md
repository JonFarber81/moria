# Moria Bestiary — Lore & Flavor

> Source: *The One Ring 2e — Moria: Through the Doors of Durin* (Free League, 2024), Ch. 4 "Fell Foes." Notes are lore/flavor only (no tabletop mechanics). Intended to drive a data-driven monster file (name, description, appearance, behavior, habitat) for the roguelike.

Chapter epigraphs (tone-setters):
> "There are older and fouler things than Orcs in the deep places of the world."
> "The city of Khazad-dûm may appear empty of life… but what fills its remote chambers is not mere darkness. Baleful fires light the deepest halls, and the sound of passing feet echoes across invisible passages."
> "And there were murmured hints of creatures more terrible than all these, but they had no name."

## Orcs — General

- A great many Orcs still infest Moria despite losses at the Battle of Dimrill Dale and the Battle of Five Armies. They prize the fortress for its size, strength, and the wealth of its mines — and out of sheer grudge. "Orcs hold grudges just as strongly as any Dwarf."
- **All Orcs of Moria hate sunlight** — they lose spirit in full sun and only roam the Dimrill Dale after dark.
- Tribal disorder: two or three tribes coexist and cannot even understand one another's orc-speech. They skirmish over territory; explorers can exploit the rivalries. Signs mark shifting borders.
- Territory by faction:
  - **Orcs of Moria** — Old Moria, the Dwarrowdelf, the Road, Dimrill Dale (most numerous)
  - **Orcs of Mordor** — the Mountains of Moria (the high galleries)
  - **Orcs of Udûn** — the Deeps and the Mines (fire-cult)
  - **West Moria** — uncontested no-man's-land
- Combat image: "Beyond the fire he saw swarming black figures… They brandished spears and scimitars which shone red as blood in the firelight. 'Doom, doom' rolled the drum-beats, louder and louder."

### Orcs of Moria (the common faction)
- Variant of the "Orcs of the North"; standard goblin-orcs, favored in darkness.
- Remember the defeat at the Battle of Five Armies; see Moria as a refuge from "the hated sun" where they can grow strong for revenge on the Free Peoples.
- Ruled at a distance by warlord **Gorgol son of Bolg** (dwells far north under Mount Gundabad); his field commander in Moria is **Malech One-eye**, who keeps his own forces deliberately scattered and divided through "cruel games and hollow malice."
- Swift **soft-shoed Goblin messengers** carry orders; **drums** muster the host. Each warband bears its own banner plus Gorgol's sign: **a severed bear's head**.
- **Orc Drummer** — instead of fighting, beats drums ("Drums in the Deep") to raise the alarm. Signature dread beat: *"Doom, doom."*

### Orcs of Udûn (the fire-cult)
- A fanatical fire-cult; even other Orcs think them "crazed fanatics." They **worship the Balrog**, casting sacrifices into blazing balefires in the Deeps. Some believe they've been changed into "vessels for a portion of the Bane's corrupted power."
- Appearance: no sigil; many wear **masks of leather and unwholesome materials**; all **stink of soot, ash, and burnt flesh**.
- Habitat: the lower levels and mines; centered on the Udûn-Temple and, far below, the Balrog's Throne. Constant strife over the Mithril veins.
- Their word for fire: **ghâsh**.
- High priest: **Thu the Firespeaker** — few followers, but the Balrog's favor makes them deadly.
- **Udûn-orc Fanatic** — "ready to die in battle or be sacrificed to the shadow and the flame." Never gives or asks quarter; wields a fiery torch-staff.
- **Udûn-orc Fire-touched** — warped by the Balrog, chosen by Thu; so insanely aggressive they make Fanatics "look peaceful." Unnaturally hard to kill — a mortal blow leaves them standing and half-restored.

### Orcs of Mordor (Sauron's agents)
- Sauron has long coveted Moria but never taken it — "Moria has only ever fallen from within." His messengers crept north after his return to Mordor (2951); the first reached the East-gate in 2960 and was **beheaded and cast out**.
- Include mighty **Uruks** and "honey-tongued messengers and diplomats to beguile the Orcs who resist Sauron's will." Their goal: break Malech One-eye, take Moria, catch Lórien between Dol Guldur and Moria — and ideally recruit the Balrog.
- Bear the sign of the **Lidless Eye** in battle but conceal their allegiance otherwise. Chief agent in Moria is a quisling Dwarf named **Har**; his Orc band is led by the reaver **Naglur**. Stronghold: the **Mountain Galleries** above the city.
- **Black Uruk** — large, strong Orcs picked as lieutenants/bodyguards; wield broad-bladed swords and horn bows.
- **Black Uruk Captain** — "hulking brutes with coal-black skin, clad in dark metal armour, wielding large, notched blades." Their yelled orders cow even Trolls.
- **Messenger of Lugbúrz** (Lugbúrz = Barad-dûr) — an emissary of the Eye, always on an errand; unnaturally fast; scimitar and jagged knife.

### Other named Orc-chiefs of Moria (villain names)
- **Yagul the Snatcher** — infamous slaver.
- **Hagrot the Sneak** — master of the Twenty-first Hall.
- **Granny Goblin** — underworld matriarch.
- **The Mauler of the Armouries**.

## Dwarves as Foes

Framing verse:
> "The mountain smoked beneath the moon; / The dwarves, they heard the tramp of doom. / They fled their hall to dying fall / Beneath his feet, beneath the moon."

Origin: some Dwarves were trapped in the lower mines when the Balrog arose, "enslaved and tormented by Durin's Bane and forced to labour in the Deeps." Their children were "born in fire and darkness." Goblin raiders add more over the centuries, prizing Dwarven smith-craft.

- **Slave Dwarf** — suffers a collective delusion: when horror grows unbearable, they imagine themselves back in Khazad-dûm as it was, "mistaking broken ruins for magnificent halls, imagining crystal lamps driving away the darkness." (See also *Moria-madness* in atmosphere notes.) Habitat: the Deeps, forges, slave-pits.
- **Dwarven Thrall** — the most crazed Slave Dwarves; fling themselves into battle *for* their Goblin tormentors, "believing themselves to be fighting for the defence of Khazad-dûm." Armed with jagged knives.
- **Dwarven Haunt** (undead/ghost) — may be evil spirits counterfeiting Dwarves, or the residue of Dwarven curse-magic set to ward their home. **Invisible to mortal eyes** — the perceptive see them as "shadows that are somehow heavy and substantial, like thick tar." They cling to objects (draining the bearer's will), **shut doors, snuff torches, hold exits closed**. Challenged by a Dwarf of strong spirit, a haunt manifests as a "slimy shadow," which a blazing torch or enchanted weapon can dispel. Victims who fall to it develop obsessions — hoarding treasure, or completing some unfinished task.

## The Balrog of Moria — "Durin's Bane"

> "What it was could not be seen: it was like a great shadow, in the middle of which was a dark form, of man-shape maybe, yet greater; and a power and terror seemed to be in it and to go before it."

- A **divine spirit** of the same order as the Wizards or Sauron — one of the Balrogs who followed Morgoth into corruption. "A spirit of the Elder Days… it saw the world being made, and walked in the darkness before the sun ever rose. No mortal can hinder it."
- Appearance: a towering shadow wreathing a dark man-shape "yet greater"; wields a **Flaming Sword** and a **Whip of Many Thongs**; wrapped in the **Flame of Udûn** so that those who strike it in melee are burned. Radiates terror.
- **The Tale:** it fought at the siege of **Thangorodrim**, fled when the battle turned hopeless "under cover of unnatural darkness," and hid "in the tunnels dug by nameless things beneath the mountain Barazinbar." It waited ~6,000 years for a summons from Morgoth that never came. When Dwarves delved for Mithril they broke into its chamber; "in a single year it single-handedly brought low the kingdom of Khazad-dûm and the line of Durin," then returned to hiding. "It can afford to wait until it is summoned once again to war."
- Powers/dread (flavor): merely seeing it fills onlookers with dread; it crushes hope with sorcery; ordinary weapons cannot wound it (only a weapon wrought to be "Bane of the Enemy itself" truly can); its burning body scorches attackers — **but it cannot use the Flame of Udûn when immersed in water** (a rare vulnerability). It can leap across a battlefield, and cannot be intimidated.
- **Unanswered questions / mystique:** What does it want? Perhaps nothing but blind reactive hate. Or it seeks a "Door into the Void" to reach Morgoth; or bides its time to rise as "the third and final Dark Lord"; or hunts the Ring. Sauron may even fear it — without the One Ring, commanding the Balrog "there would be a good chance it might master him instead."
- **Alternate Banes** (GM variants, if you want the deepest boss to be ambiguous): the Witch-king of Angmar; an ancient wingless Dragon; a nameless primordial horror "that gnawed the foundations of stone"; a cursed weapon passed Orc-chief to Orc-chief; or "betrayal from within" — civil war mythologized into a monster.

## Other Foul Things

### Ash-wraiths
- Evil spirits **conjured by the Orcs of Udûn** — "called out of the Void," they never wore mortal flesh. Slave-pit legend says making one requires a living sacrifice hurled into the flames.
- Appearance: "faintly man-shaped clouds of whirling ash and hot sparks." — "Sparks from the flame of Udûn."
- Behavior: left unguided they "swiftly gutter out and dissipate." Bound to guard temples or mustered in swarms for battle — "fearsome when encountered in great numbers." Their fiery touch burns; their burning aura scorches melee attackers — **but not when immersed in water**. Flavor: *"Ash! Ash and smoke blown away on the wind!"*

### Cave Bats
- Many kinds dwell in the Misty Mountains, "mostly evil beasts, allied with the Goblins."
  - Small long-eared bats nest in Moria's air-vents and upper halls — a useful sign, since hidden cave-mouths can be found where they emerge at twilight.
  - Swarms of tiny bats swirl through Dimrill Dale by night; a bite "can sicken even a mighty warrior."
- **Great Carrion Bat** — invaded the empty halls after the Fall and fed on the corpses of slain Dwarves; now favor rotten flesh and **accompany Goblin armies "in expectation of a banquet."** Despite their size they attack only weakened or exhausted prey. Cowardly, hate sunlight, wind-fast and hard to hit. Flavor: "the bat-cloud came, flying lower… and whirled above them shutting out the light and filling them with dread."

### Marrow-eaters
- Known only from a handful of wild tales; the Dwarves met them "in the deepest portions of the Mines, far beneath Caradhras," only in the last years before the Balrog's advent.
- Appearance: thin-limbed, human in size and shape, with **skin like a fish that glimmers with its own pale luminescence**, great bulging eyes with no iris or pupil, and **long sticky fingers and long tongues for sucking marrow from bones**. They wield **knives of polished obsidian**, make no sound, and smear a **glowing liquid on the rocks to draw strange sigils**.
- Behavior: said to have **no skeletons themselves**, able to squeeze through the smallest gap in the rock. They capture prisoners and carry them to lairs in the depths, "so that every scrap of skin, meat and marrow can be savoured."

### Stone Toads
- Huge toads of the wet caves of Moria (e.g. the Gardens of Thrym Thistlebeard); some grow big enough to swallow a Hobbit whole.
- Their skin secretes poisonous slime; as they age their hide hardens **until it is hard as stone**, slime oozing through cracks. Standing still, they are "almost indistinguishable from ordinary rocks," and animate rapidly to attack those who disturb their pools. Territorial.
- **Toadstone:** when a toad grows old enough, a gemstone forms on its brow. Toadstones have magical healing properties; many ancient Dwarven talismans of good health contain them. *(Good roguelike loot from a monster drop.)*

### Tappers
- "Tales of Tappers are as old as the First Hall — and yet, in all that time, no Dwarf has ever laid eyes on a Tapper." May be spirits, ghosts, goblin-mischief, or "some subterranean folk from the realms below."
- Named for the sound they make — "a distant hammering or tapping on the stone that echoes through the passageways." Each Tapper has its own rhythm and "rules over a particular portion of the underworld." (Not unique to Moria — miners in the mountains of Gondor tell tales of tapping too.)
- **Cruel or kindly:** some warn of impending cave-ins; others lure explorers to their doom or betray them to Goblins. They delight in offerings of food and precious things; some love songs and tap along, others despise anything that interrupts the tapping. Flavor: *"there came out of the depths faint knocks: tom-tap, tom-tap."*

### Cave Dogs of Moria
- Strange hairless dogs lurk in the depths; their ancestors were guard-dogs, turnspit-dogs, and ratters, now "all one breed, one pack." They never bark — only high-pitched yelps. No real threat, but opportunistic scavengers that trail heroes to eat whatever's left. (Descended from the small guard-dogs the Dwarves bred against rats in the storerooms.)
