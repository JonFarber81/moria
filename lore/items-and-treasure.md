# Moria — Items, Treasure & Mithril

> Source: *TOR2 — Moria: Through the Doors of Durin*, Appendix ("Balin's Expedition," "On Mithril") plus named treasures surfaced across Ch. 5. Lore/flavor only — mechanical Value/bonus ratings omitted. This is the reference for a themed loot table and for the game's backstory.

## Backstory — Balin's Expedition (the game's premise)

The player is a lone Dwarf in the shadow of **Balin's doomed recolonisation**. As long as the Balrog walks the Halls of Durin, retaking the city is impossible — the expedition is "almost certainly doomed."

- **Balin son of Fundin** — of Thorin's Company on the Quest of Erebor; later a chief advisor to King Dáin. Hungry for a kingdom of his own, he championed retaking Moria for years until Dáin, against his better judgement, relented.
- He set out from Erebor with **Óin**, **Ori**, and "a great many Dwarves," wintering amid the ruins of Dimrill Dale.
- **Tale of Years of Balin's reign:**
  - Drove Orcs from the East-gate; established in the **Twenty-first Hall**; split the host into three Companies (guard the Dale camp / restore safe Moria / delve deeper).
  - **Balin crowned Lord of Moria** on Durin's Day, on a throne in the Twenty-first Hall.
  - **"The Jaws Close":** Gundabad Goblins overwhelm the Dimrill Dale camp by night; survivors retreat into Moria. **Óin sent west to seek the West-gate.**
  - **"The Fall of Balin":** messengers to the Beornings ambushed; scouts find the West-gate blocked by a lake and a monster (the **Watcher in the Water**). The Dwarves discover **Mithril and treasures in a strangely burnt section** of the city. The Shadow lies heavy on Balin; he goes to the **Mirrormere** to seek the ancestors' wisdom — and **an Orc slays him there.**
  - **"They Are Coming…":** the Orcs capture the Second Hall, cutting the Dwarves off from sunlight. "Drums sounded in the deep. They are coming…"
- **Backstory hook — the hunt for Thráin's Ring:** Glóin: "it was partly in hope to find that ring that Balin went away." The last of the Seven Dwarf-rings was taken from Thráin at Dol Guldur — recovering it may have been the price of Dáin's blessing.

## Mithril ("true-silver," "Moria-silver")

*"Mithril! All folk desired it. It could be beaten like copper, and polished like glass… light and yet harder than tempered steel. Its beauty was like to that of common silver, but the beauty of Mithril did not tarnish or grow dim."*

- Found nowhere but the Blessed Realm, Númenor, and Moria; none newly mined by Elf or Dwarf in a thousand years. **Astoundingly light** — even raw native mithril is worth ~5× its weight; a treasure of mithril adds almost nothing to a bearer's burden. (Great as a "precious but weightless" loot tier.)

### Forms / species of mithril (an item-tier ladder)
- **Native Mithril** — the raw form: nuggets of pure metal, must be washed and sorted; beatable and polishable but **soft, no harder than copper**.
- **True Mithril** — native mithril processed to keep its beauty while making it **much stronger**; the base of all rarer alloys. The Dwarves remember the technique but lack a supply; the **forges of Mordor** now remake smuggled Moria-mithril into it.
- **Ithildin** — reflects only **starlight and moonlight**; "slumbers until touched by one who speaks words long forgotten." Made into ink for **moon-letters** (as on the Doors of Durin).
- **Erceleb** — Ithildin's dark cousin: it **drinks in** silvery light; dull grey by day, pitch-black in moonlight. Used to mark hidden doors underground (glows brilliantly when "awoken"), and can **capture memories** — words spoken to it in moonlight replay when the metal is awakened.
- **Mithril-steel** — steel veined with hair-thin mithril threads "like veins in a leaf," greatly strengthened; looks like ordinary steel **except under the full moon**, when the veins show.
- **Thilevril** — mithril drawn into nearly invisible, immensely strong wire; used for **harp-strings** of unearthly range (Orcs twist it into cruel whips), and rarely for bow-strings or tripwires.
- **Sullied Mithril** — "a new devilry out of Mordor": becomes **hotter at a word of command**, letting a weapon burn like a firebrand or burst into flame.

### Named mithril gear
- **Mithril Helm** — dwarven-forged, effectively weightless (cf. the Mithril helms of the Guards of Minas Tirith).
- **Mithril Shield / buckler** — weightless; usable even alongside a two-handed weapon.
- **Mithril Weapon** — drastically lightened dwarven blade.

## Named Treasures, Artifacts & Heirlooms

Great as roguelike loot names / vault labels:

- **Durin's Axe** — legendary axe of the Kings, forged in the Elder Days by **Aulë the Smith**; wielded in the defence of the city. If it survives, it lies in the **Lower Armoury** of the Third Deep.
- **The Last Ring of the Dwarves** — the unfinished ring in the dead hand of Master-smith **Fraegr** in the Lost Citadel; meant as a weapon against the Balrog, to imbue its wearer with protection against fire and darkness (three rubies still waiting to be set). Coupled with Durin's Axe it could "raise a mortal hero to the status of legend."
- **The Phial of Galadriel** — a light that alone might drive back the Shadow even in the Balrog's Temple to Darkness, where all other light is quenched.
- **The Ring of Keys** — a Mithril circle of ornate keys (in the Cairn of King Náin) that unlocks all the Doors of the Dwarrowdelf, the right key always to hand; cannot be lost or stolen; looking through the circle reveals hidden doors. Named keys include the East-gate, the Dimrill Door ("17"), the Vault of Uruktharbun, and the Royal Armoury.
- **Thrym's Eye** — a sapphire "eye" necklace from Thrym's Tomb; grants far-sight and lets a sleeper spy on the cave through the statue's eyes.
- **Toadstone** — a gemstone that forms on the brow of the eldest **Stone Toads**; has magical **healing** properties; the core of many ancient Dwarven talismans of good health. *(A natural monster-drop consumable.)*
- **The Rings of Nyi** — cursed rings that progressively **overwrite the wearer's mind** until they become the long-dead Queen Nyi (replacement, not possession). Dangerous cautionary loot.
- **Lesser rings of invisibility** — three gold rings found in West Moria, now borne by the unseen **Invisible Goblins** of Goblin Village.
- **The Annals of the Gwaith-i-Mirdáin** — the Jewel-smiths' library: first-hand accounts of the making of the Rings of Power (told by Celebrimbor and the "Lord of Gifts," Sauron). "Remarkably dry and flammable."

## Vault / Hoard Names (evocative labels for treasure levels or chests)

- **Vault of Uruktharbun** — the fabled Treasure Vault of Khazad-dûm (King's Coffers, Mithril Vault, Vault of Treasures — or a decoy death-trap).
- **The Lower Armoury of the Third Deep** — the martial treasures of the Dwarves (Durin's Axe).
- **The Imperishable Hall** — a hall of Ithildin mirrors that "time cannot ruin."
- **The Cairn of King Náin** — honored tomb of the last king of Moria (grave-goods; the Ring of Keys).
- **The Last Redoubt / Fifth Hall** — the sealed final-stand fortress and arsenal.
- **The Heart of the Mountain** — the tomb of Durin the Deathless (a place of power, not plunder).

## Ancient Dwarf-Houses (deep background)

- **Aulë the Smith** — one of the Valar, maker of the Dwarves; runic prayers to him line the Endless Stair (which he may even have made).
- **Nogrod and Belegost** — the ancient dwarf-cities of Beleriand (home of the master-smith **Telchar**), shattered when Beleriand drowned; their exiled nobles fled to Moria and, without prestige there, dwelt resentfully in the Lower Deeps (the "Houses of the Exiles," now mockingly named by Goblins).
