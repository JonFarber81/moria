# Moria — Names, History & Timeline

> Source: *TOR2 — Moria: Through the Doors of Durin*, Ch. 1–2. Background/naming reference for a later content pass. (Per project scope: this is lore reference, not a directive to build narrative systems now.)

## Names of the Place

- **Khazad-dûm** — the Dwarvish name; "the eldest and greatest of the fortresses of Elves or Men or Dwarves in Middle-earth." Invoked with reverence: *"Khazad-dûm! Next to it, the cities of Gondor are but newly built!"*
- **Moria** — "Black Pit" in Elvish; originally used by the Elves of Hollin only for the mines and west road, later for the whole city after the lamps went out (post-TA 1980).
- **Dwarrowdelf** — the "City of the Dwarves," the city proper, as distinct from the **Mines of Moria**. Dwarves are precise and take offence when the terms are misapplied.
- **The Black Pit** — common epithet, spoken with dread.
- **"Wonder of the Northern World"** — the city in its glory: *"Moria! Moria! Wonder of the Northern world! Too deep we delved there, and woke the nameless fear."*

## Geography & Vertical Structure

*(This is the key reference for naming dungeon depths.)*

- The Dwarrowdelf lies mostly beneath **Mount Silvertine (Celebdil)**, eastern Misty Mountains.
- **East-gate** (Dimrill Gate / Dimrill Stair side) and **West-gate** (Hollin Gate / Doors of Durin, near Elven **Eregion/Hollin**). East-to-west spans ~40 miles; mines run ~20 miles north, ~10 south.
- **Mirrormere** — the sacred lake Durin discovered.
- Vertical reckoning:
  - The **Level of the Gates / First Level** is the East-gate baseline.
  - Floors **above** are **Levels** (Second, Third … up to the **Twelfth Level**).
  - Floors **below** are **Deeps** (First Deep, Second Deep … down to the **Ninth Deep**).
  - Tunnels and outposts ran higher and deeper still.
- Dwarves **numbered rather than named** their halls — e.g. "the Twenty-First Hall on the Seventh Level" — to convey the staggering scale.
- **The Deeps** and **the Mines** are the deepest, most dangerous zones.

## Peoples & Key Persons

- **Durin I, "the Deathless"** — eldest of Dwarves, "woke and walked alone," founder of the Dwarrowdelf; Father of the **Longbeards** (Durin's Folk). Line: Durin I → Durin VI (slain by the Balrog) → prophesied **Durin VII, last of his name**, who may reclaim Moria in the Fourth Age.
- **The Seven Houses of the Dwarves**, each from one of the seven fathers; Longbeards eldest. Western houses dwelt at **Nogrod and Belegost** (Beleriand); after Beleriand drowned, many migrated east to Moria.
- **Aulë the Smith** — one of the Valar, maker of the Dwarves; runic prayers to him line the Endless Stair.
- Persons named: Náin I, Thráin I, **Thrór** (slain on the doorstep by **Azog**), **Thráin II** (captured at Dol Guldur, Ring taken), **Thorin**, **Balin son of Fundin**, **Dáin Ironfoot (Dáin II)**, **Nár** (Thrór's servant), **Narvi** (the smith who made the West-gate), **Celebrimbor** (Elven ring-smith of Eregion).
- Wizard names: **Tharkun** (Dwarvish for Gandalf/Mithrandir); **Saruman**.

## A Tale of Years of the City

### First Age
- **Khazad-dûm founded by Durin I**, and prospers while the outer world is wracked by war.

### Second Age
- **c. 1** — A Balrog of Morgoth flees the wrath of the Valar after Angband's fall and hides under the mountains.
- **c. 40** — Dwarves fleeing the ruin of the western cities (Nogrod/Belegost) take refuge in Khazad-dûm.
- **c. 750** — High Elves settle **Eregion**, build **Ost-in-Edhil**, found **Hollin**; trade with the Dwarves for **Mithril**; the two peoples become the greatest craftsmen east of the Sea.
- **c. 1000** — The citadel of **West Moria** established.
- **c. 1500** — The **Rings of Power** forged (Sauron in disguise aiding). Dwarven tradition: Celebrimbor gave Durin the first of the Seven before the One was made.
- **c. 1600** — Sauron forges the **One Ring**; war begins.
- **1697** — Eregion destroyed; Moria's doors closed, city besieged.
- **2251** — The Nine Ringwraiths first appear.
- **3441** — Last Alliance defeats Sauron; **Durin IV** leads a small Dwarf-army; Isildur claims the One Ring.

### Third Age
- **c. 1300** — Orcs attack the Dwarves; the realm of **Angmar** arises.
- **1980** — **The Balrog awakens and slays Durin VI.** ("Too deep we delved… and woke the nameless fear.")
- **1981** — **Náin I slain; the Dwarves flee; Moria is lost.** Thráin I escapes bearing **Durin's Ring**.
- **1999** — Thráin I founds the Kingdom under the Mountain (Erebor).
- **c. 2480** — Orcs, directed by Sauron, defile Moria. "Moria becomes known as a place of uttermost dread and terror."
- **c. 2700** — **Azog** claims Moria.
- **2790** — Thrór returns to Moria and is **slain on the doorstep by Azog**.
- **2793–2799** — The **War of the Dwarves and Orcs**, ending at the **Battle of Azanulbizar (Nanduhirion)** in Dimrill Dale: a costly Dwarven victory, half their number slain; **Dáin slays Azog**. Dáin warns Thráin off Moria: *"the world must change, and some other power than ours must come before Durin's Folk walk again in Moria."*
- **2841** — Thráin II captured in Mirkwood, imprisoned in **Dol Guldur**; his Ring taken.
- **2848–2850** — Gandalf (Tharkun) enters Moria seeking Thráin; passes east-to-west; later finds Thráin in Dol Guldur.
- **2941** — Erebor restored; **Battle of Five Armies**; Bolg son of Azog slain; Dáin becomes King under the Mountain.
- **~2965** — **"The present day"** of the sourcebook: with Goblins scattered after Erebor, some risk entering Moria again — "the race for Khazad-dûm."
- **2980** — **Aragorn** enters Moria by the East-gate. *"I too once passed the Dimrill Gate… the memory is very evil."*
- **2989** — **Balin leads his expedition to reclaim Moria.** (See `items-and-treasure.md` for the expedition's fate.)
- **2994** — **Recorded death of Balin** in the Book of Mazarbul.
- **3018** — Gollum enters Moria to hide.
- **3019** — The Fellowship passes through; Gandalf destroys Durin's Bridge and falls with the Balrog.

> For more than six thousand years Moria was the mansion of Durin's folk; abandoned a thousand years ago; abode of Orcs almost as long.

## Present-Day Situation (~2965) — Why a Lone Dwarf Descends

- Most Misty-Mountain Orcs were scattered/destroyed at the Battle of Five Armies. Whispers run "from the Iron Hills to the Blue Mountains": *might Khazad-dûm be retaken?*
- **Dáin Ironfoot forbids** his own followers to reclaim Moria — he alone "looked beyond the shadow of the gate" and "sensed something of Durin's Bane." His word binds Durin's Folk but not other Dwarves or treasure-hunters — so begins "the race for Khazad-dûm."
- Reasons people enter: **treasure**; **Mithril**; the **Perilous Path** (crossing the mountains / hiding from the Eye); **vengeance** (against an Orc-chief); **Ring-lore** (fragments of Eregion's ring-craft preserved in the Lost Citadel of the Ring-smiths / Chambers of Narvi).
